# Ideal PFR Model Overview: Haber-Bosch Synthesis

*This document summarizes the mathematical assumptions of the current reactor simulation. It is designed to serve as a baseline for expanding the model to account for non-ideal flow and Residence Time Distribution (RTD).*

## 1. Reactor Topology & Flow Network
- **Configuration:** The design consists of **three sequential adiabatic packed beds**.
- **Spatial Coordinate:** We integrate over catalyst weight ($W$) from $0 \to 2500$ kg per bed, rather than reactor volume ($V$). 
- **Inter-stage Cooling:** Between each bed, the gas is perfectly instantaneously cooled by a fixed $\Delta T$ of $60$ K without any change in volume, pressure, or conversion (a theoretical idealized heat exchanger).

## 2. Ideal Plug Flow Assumptions (To Be Replaced by RTD)
Currently, our standard PFR assumes perfectly ideal flow. For your non-ideal analysis, here is exactly what is currently assumed and what you will need to change:

- **No Axial Dispersion ($D_a = 0$):** We assume zero back-mixing or forward-diffusion along the axis. Gas moves purely via convection. 
  - *Non-Ideal Upgrade:* You will need to switch from a 1st-order ODE to a 2nd-order ODE by adding an axial dispersion term ($D_a \frac{d^2 X}{dz^2}$) or model the beds as a cascade of Continuous Stirred-Tank Reactors (Tanks-in-Series Model).
- **Uniform Flat Velocity Profile:** We assume gas doesn't stick to the walls. The velocity is identical across the entire radial cross-section.
  - *Non-Ideal Upgrade:* Real packed beds suffer from channeling and boundary layer effects. You can introduce an $E(t)$ curve to model velocity bypassing.
- **The Current RTD:** Because of the two assumptions above, the current Residence Time Distribution $E(t)$ is simply a **Dirac Delta Function** $\delta(t - \tau)$. Every single nitrogen molecule spends exactly the same amount of time in the bed.

## 3. Kinetic & Thermodynamic Assumptions
- **Kinetics:** We use the Temkin-Pyzhev formulation. The model computes bulk partial pressures assuming ideal gas stoichiometry ($P_i = y_i P_{total}$). 
- **Isobaric Operation:** We assume a strict uniform pressure of $200$ atm. The Ergun Equation is **not** implemented, meaning we currently ignore any momentum loss/pressure drops across the 2500 kg of packed catalyst.
- **Adiabatic Energy Balance:** Within each bed, $100\%$ of the heat generated by the exothermic ammonia synthesis goes directly into the sensible heat of the flowing gas mixture. There are zero heat losses through the reactor walls.

## 4. How the Code is Built for Modification
The solver uses `scipy.integrate.solve_ivp` to step through the ODEs. 
If you plan to use an RTD function $E(t)$ to calculate the real non-ideal conversion ($X_{real} = \int_{0}^{\infty} X_{ideal}(t) E(t) dt$), you can translate our current spatial $W$-based conversion array `X_all` into a time-domain trajectory $X(t)$ and perform the convolution!
